Xu Beihong

On July 19, 1895, Xu Beihong, a generation of Chinese painting master, was born in Jiangsu.

Xu Beihong began to learn painting from his father when he was 9 years old. He copied Wu Youru's paintings after lunch every day and learned painting skills such as color matching and color setting. At the age of 10, he was able to help his father fill in the secondary part of the picture, and write couplets such as "Shihe Shitai, Happy Birthday" for the villagers.

In 1919, he went to France to study. He was admitted to the National School of Fine Arts in Paris to study oil painting and sketch. He also traveled to Western European countries to observe and study western art. With the help of the Beiyang government, Xu Beihong, 24, went to France to study painting. At the beginning of his arrival in Europe, he visited the British Museum, the National Gallery, the Royal College Exhibition and the Louvre Palace Art Museum in France, witnessing a large number of outstanding works since the Renaissance.

After the founding of New China in 1949, Xu Beihong, while serving as the government and administrative work, continued to write and create, and enthusiastically described the new people, new things and new features in the construction of New China. He painted heroes in battle, went to the construction site of the water conservancy project in Daoshu, Shandong Province to experience life, and collected bits and pieces of materials reflecting the construction of New China for the portraits of model workers and migrant workers. Unfortunately, all these artistic activities ended abruptly because the painter left the world prematurely.
