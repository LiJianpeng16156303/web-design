Wounded lion

The Lion of Injury is a Chinese painting created by Xu Beihong during the Anti-Japanese War.Paper; 110 cm in length and 109 cm in breadth; made in 1938; hidden in private.

In the New Year of 1938, the situation of China's Anti-Japanese War became more and more tense. In early January, Xu Beihong, then chairman of the Art Department of the Central University, and Wu Yunrui, a close friend, lived in Chongqing. When discussing the situation of refugees in China, Xu Beihong touched upon the situation and wrote a passionate work with infinite meaning, The Lion of Injury, and wrote an inscription in the upper right corner of the painting to record the work. An unusual experience of birth.

Confucius was in urgent need of a national disaster. He visited Chongqing with Mr. Lin Ruo at times and did not hesitate to talk about it. This is a masterpiece combining realism with romanticism. China is known as the "sleeping lion" of the East, and is now occupied by Japanese imperialism in most of northeastern China. The "sleeping lion" has become a wounded male lion. The wounded lion with glaring eyes is ready for battle and struggle in an irretrievable look. It contains strength and strength.
